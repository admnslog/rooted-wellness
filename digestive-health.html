Clinical Specialization

Digestive
Disorders

Good digestion is essential for breaking down meals and absorbing nutrients that support overall health. Digestive disorders can begin at a young age and persist for decades, often causing discomfort that impacts quality of life, social interactions, and emotional wellbeing.

"The gut is the foundation of health — treating it requires both science and care."

What's normal?

Clarity is the first step toward healing. We use clinical markers to define true digestive health.

calendar_today

Frequency

Ideally, 1–3 well-formed, easy-to-pass bowel movements per day, often after meals — free from chronic gas, bloating, or discomfort.

Signs of Poor Digestion

Digestive issues can present in many ways: constipation, diarrhea, acid reflux, abdominal pain, bloating, gas, slow digestion, belching, nausea, vomiting, blood or mucus in the stool, dark or floating stools, pain after eating, indigestion, and hemorrhoids.
